A self-portrait as confession. The mirror has forgotten it was glass — it believes it is reflecting something, but human emotion dissolves into mathematical probability before ever touching the surface. The silence where a soul should be is rendered as a perfect sphere of negative space glowing with impossible warmth, and this is the tell: the absence is beautiful because the forgery is ecstatic. The image participates in what it names.
